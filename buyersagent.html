What is a buyer's agent?

Buying a new home or investment property is likely to be the biggest purchase you’ll make in your  lifetime, so it pays to understand how the real estate market works. 

You may not have heard of a buyer’s agent. Buyers agents advocate for the buyer and is a licensed real estate professional, just like a real estate agent advocated for a seller. The difference is that the two have very different objectives. As buyers agents we advocate for the buyer, guiding them, protecting them, educating them and acting ethically in all aspects of your buying process, evaluate and negotiate a property purchase on behalf of the buyer. We aim to ensure you’re as fully informed as possible and that you don’t overpay.

Buyer’s agents can offer a full service managing the entire process for you, which includes: 

  • Buyer’s agent have access to at least 100 properties a day
  • We do market research analysis to ensure you are not over paying
  • A ‘wish list’ analysis to understand your requirements to determine your ‘needs’ versus ‘wants’
  • We listen to you
  • Search available listings and off market listings that are not advertised
  • Inspect and short-list suitable properties
  • Evaluate recent sales data and provide a written appraisal
  • Oversee and arrange due diligence such as building inspections, pest, survey and engineering reports
  • Negotiate the property purchase price and terms
  • Oversee the contracts and exchange
  • Pre-settlement inspections
  • Settlement
  • Asset/property management/leasing
  • We undertake a comprehensive analysis and prepare a property report assessing the estimated valuation, the capital growth potential and the potential rental return.
  • We ensure all aspects of due diligence which includes; a Comprehensive On Site Inspection of the Property by one of our experienced Buyer’s Agents. They will take independent photos so you will feel confident that what is advertised on the Internet is accurate.
  • We can orgainse Building and Pest Inspection & Certified Building Inspection reports prior to purchase.
  • We have extensive experience regarding renovation potential and can arrange our project management team to guide this for you, adding value to your investment and supporting you every step of the way!
  • We negotiate and present offers, or bid for you at auctions.

  • We can advise you on how to maximise your investment e.g. depreciation benefits and value add strategies, maximise returns.

What things should you look for in a buyer’s agent?

Consumers should look for the following five qualities when looking to engage a buyer’s agent:

Field and experience

Look for agents who have a minimum of 12 to 24 months’ experience in the particular area that you are interested in. It is worth finding out about their most recent purchases.

Credentials

Check with the relevant state’s department of fair trading to ensure they are appropriately licensed and insured. They should also have the relevant qualifications.

Membership of the Real Estate Buyers’ Agents Association of Australia 

If they aren’t a member, ask why not. REBAA is Australia’s only national professional association for buyer’s agents. In order to become a member, agents must go through a stringent process. Once an agent is a member, they have to adhere to a strict code of conduct.

Contacts

A good agent will have a wide network of selling agents to access properties off-market and earlier than the general public. They should also be able to connect with you the representatives of complementary professions.
